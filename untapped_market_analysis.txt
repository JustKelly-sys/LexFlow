1. The Convergence of Attention Economies: Where
"MemeFi" Meets "InfoFi"
The trajectory of decentralized finance in the mid-2020s has been defined by two distinct yet
structurally converging phenomena: the hyper-financialization of culture through
"memecoins" and the financialization of truth through prediction markets. While these sectors
appear superficially distinct—one driven by humor and community signaling, the other by
probability and geopolitical forecasting—their underlying market mechanics and user
behaviors are rapidly aligning. This convergence presents a distinct, high-value opportunity
for software developers and market architects: the creation of a sophisticated "Bloomberg
Terminal" for the prediction economy, modeled directly on the battle-tested, high-velocity
tooling infrastructure that currently powers the memecoin ecosystem.
The current state of Polymarket, the dominant decentralized prediction market platform,
mirrors the early days of the Uniswap era before the advent of sophisticated aggregators and
analytics suites. Volumes have exploded to billions of dollars, driven by high-stakes political
events and cultural moments, yet the interface between the trader and the protocol remains
largely largely manual and opaque.1 Traders navigate complex conditional token structures
with tools that offer little more than basic price history, leaving them exposed to information
asymmetry that sophisticated actors—"whales" and market makers—ruthlessly exploit.
In the adjacent memecoin sector, the tooling standard has evolved into a predator-prey
ecosystem where speed, visual forensics, and automated execution are prerequisites for
survival. Platforms like Photon, BullX, and Bubble Maps have democratized access to "insider"
data, allowing retail traders to visualize wallet clusters, snipe liquidity launches in milliseconds,
and detect wash trading instantly.2 The user query identifies a critical vacuum: the lack of
equivalent, high-performance tooling for Polymarket.
This report posits that the next "unicorn" application in the crypto-analytics space will not be
another exchange, but an "Alpha Terminal" for prediction markets that ports the aggressive,
forensic, and automated features of memecoin tools to the Conditional Tokens Framework
(CTF). By treating outcome shares not just as bets, but as volatile assets subject to the same
laws of supply concentration, insider accumulation, and liquidity manipulation as memecoins,
developers can unlock a massive untapped market for paid, premium services.

1.1 The Thesis of Adjacency
The core thesis driving this analysis is "structural adjacency." Memecoins and prediction
market shares share three fundamental characteristics that necessitate identical tooling
architectures:
1. Binary Outcomes: A memecoin is often a binary bet on the virality of a ticker; it either
goes to zero (rug) or "moons." Similarly, a Polymarket position settles mathematically to
$1.00 or $0.00. This binary nature creates extreme volatility as settlement approaches,
requiring tools that can visualize "max pain" and probability cliffs.4
2. Information Asymmetry: In memecoins, "snipers" buy the block a token is launched. In
prediction markets, "insiders" buy the "Yes" share minutes before a news story breaks.5
Both require tools that monitor mempools and on-chain events for the earliest signs of
informed accumulation.
3. Visual Due Diligence: Memecoin traders use bubble maps to see if a dev controls 40%
of the supply. Prediction market traders need (but lack) tools to see if a single whale
controls 40% of the "Yes" side, creating an artificial consensus.6
The untapped market lies in bridging this gap: building a "Photon for Truth" that offers the
speed of a sniper bot and the forensic depth of a bubble map, tailored specifically for the
ERC-1155 Conditional Tokens that power Polymarket.
2. Market Architecture: The Technical Foundation of
Opportunity
To build superior tools, one must first understand the obscure and often misunderstood
architecture of Polymarket. Unlike the standard ERC-20 tokens that populate the memecoin
world, Polymarket relies on the Gnosis Conditional Tokens Framework (CTF), a complexity that
breaks most standard portfolio trackers and creates the very opportunity for specialized, paid
software.
2.1 The Conditional Tokens Framework (CTF) and ERC-1155
The fundamental asset on Polymarket is not a simple token, but a conditional claim. When a
user deposits collateral (USDC), it is split into two or more outcome shares (e.g., YES and NO)
using the CTF smart contract.7 These shares are compliant with the ERC-1155 multi-token
standard, allowing for batch transfers and gas-efficient operations.
However, this structure creates a "data blindness" for generalist tools. Standard block
explorers and wallet trackers often fail to correctly parse PositionIDs. A PositionID is derived
mathematically from the underlying collateral token and a CollectionID, which is in turn
derived from the ConditionID and an IndexSet.7 This recursive hashing means that "YES"
shares for two different markets are entirely distinct assets with no obvious on-chain link

unless the tool is specifically engineered to decode the ancillary data from the UMA oracle.8
For a developer, this is the first moat. A paid service that can seamlessly decode and display a
user's net exposure across complex, merged positions offers immediate value over free,
generalist trackers that display unintelligible ERC-1155 IDs.
2.2 The Hybrid CLOB and Off-Chain Matching
Polymarket's efficiency comes from its hybrid Central Limit Order Book (CLOB). Unlike the
Automated Market Makers (AMMs) like Uniswap that power memecoins, Polymarket matches
orders off-chain via a relayer system before settling them on-chain.9
● The Mechanism: Users sign orders using EIP-712 (typed structured data), which are sent
to the Polymarket operator. The operator matches a buy and a sell, and then submits the
trade to the Polygon blockchain.10
● The Tooling Implication: Relying solely on on-chain data (OrderFilled events) introduces
a latency of seconds or minutes—an eternity in high-frequency trading. The "untapped
market" for snipers lies in connecting to the off-chain CLOB via WebSocket to see the
order book state before it settles on-chain. This allows a tool to visualize "walls" of
liquidity and spoofing attempts that are invisible to on-chain observers.11
2.3 The Double-Counting Trap
A critical nuance for analytics tools is the issue of volume verification. Common analytical
queries often sum up all OrderFilled events, leading to a massive overestimation of
volume—often double the real figure.12 This occurs because a single trade emits events for
both the maker and the taker. A high-quality, paid analytics dashboard must implement
sophisticated deduplication logic, filtering for OrdersMatched events or specific maker/taker
flags to provide "clean" data. This accuracy becomes a selling point: "The only dashboard
with real volume data."
3. The Memecoin Standard: Benchmarking the
"Adjacent" Features
To define the product requirements for a premium Polymarket tool, we must analyze the
feature sets of market-leading memecoin tools. These platforms have established the user
expectations for "Alpha" tools.
3.1 The "Photon" Model: Speed and Integration
Photon is a terminal that integrates charts, wallet management, and execution into a single
view.
● Key Feature: "One-Click Buy." It generates a local wallet for the user, bypassing the slow
MetaMask approval pop-ups.

● Polymarket Equivalent: The "Event Sniper." A terminal that uses a Polymarket Proxy
Wallet (Gnosis Safe) but streamlines the signing process, allowing users to "snipe" a
mispriced outcome the millisecond a market is created.
3.2 The "Bubble Maps" Model: Visual Forensics
Bubble Maps visualizes the connections between wallets to detect "Sybil" attacks (one person
pretending to be many).2
● Key Feature: Clustering. If Wallet A funds Wallets B, C, D, and E, and they all buy the
same token, Bubble Maps draws a cluster.
● Polymarket Equivalent: "Consensus Forensics." If a political market shows a 60%
chance for a candidate, but 80% of the "YES" money comes from a single cluster of
connected wallets, the market is manipulated. Visualizing this is the "killer feature" for a
paid Polymarket auditing tool.3
3.3 The "DexScreener" Model: Filters and Discovery
DexScreener allows users to filter 10,000 new tokens by liquidity, volume, and age.
● Key Feature: "New Pairs" filter.
● Polymarket Equivalent: "New Markets Feed." Polymarket launches dozens of markets
daily. A tool that filters them by "Liquidity > $10k" and "Spread < 1 cent" allows market
makers to find the most efficient venues instantly.13
4. Exhaustive Audit of the Current Landscape
Despite the capital influx, the current Polymarket tooling landscape is rudimentary, resembling
"Web 1.0" static pages rather than "Web 3.0" dynamic applications.
4.1 Wallet Trackers: The Noise Problem
Existing trackers like PolyWhaler and various unnamed Telegram bots provide alerts on large
trades.14
● The Deficiency: They lack context. A whale buying "NO" on a candidate might be
hedging a "YES" position held in a different wallet, or purely arbitraging a price difference
against another platform like Kalshi. Current trackers report this as "Bearish Sentiment,"
misleading the user.
● User Feedback: Reddit users explicitly request "Wallet profiles with past performance...
and consistency".15 They want to know if a wallet is a "Smart Whale" (high win rate) or a
"Lucky Gambler" (high variance).
4.2 Analytics Dashboards: The Latency Problem
Platforms like HashDive and various Dune Dashboards offer macro-level statistics.5

● The Deficiency: These are largely retrospective. They tell you what happened yesterday.
They do not offer real-time, tick-by-tick order book visualization (Level 2 data) which is
essential for day trading news events.
● Visual Gap: There is no tool that visualizes the "Depth Chart" effectively, showing where
the buy/sell walls are located for specific outcome shares.17
4.3 Execution Interfaces: The Manual Friction
Trading on the native Polymarket interface involves navigating categories, clicking into
markets, and signing transactions.
● The Deficiency: There is no "Hot Key" trading or "Ladder" interface (common in futures
trading) that allows a user to place multiple limit orders at staggered prices instantly.
5. Blueprint I: The Untapped "Visual Forensics" Market
The most significant opportunity for a paid service is a "Forensic Truth Engine"—essentially,
Bubble Maps for prediction markets. This tool addresses the primary anxiety of prediction
market traders: manipulation by large holders.
5.1 The "Consensus Cluster" Algorithm
To build this, the tool must index the Polygon blockchain for TransferSingle and TransferBatch
events emitted by the CTF contract.
1. Ingestion: Track every wallet holding a specific PositionID (e.g., "YES on Inflation > 3%").
2. Graph Construction: Build a directed graph where nodes are wallets and edges are
historical transfers of USDC (collateral) or MATIC (gas).
3. Cluster Detection: Use community detection algorithms (e.g., Louvain method) to
identify wallets that share funding sources.
4. Visualization: Render a bubble map where the central node is the "Funder" (e.g., a
Binance hot wallet or a known whale address) and satellite nodes are the trading wallets.
The "Alpha" Insight: If a market has $10M in volume, but the "Bubble Map" shows that 70%
of the "YES" side is held by a single cluster, the "Crowd Wisdom" is fake. This is actionable
intelligence that hedge funds and serious traders will pay a premium to access
($100-$500/month).
5.2 The "Sybil Score"
Assign a "Sybil Score" to every market.
● Low Score (0-20): Organic distribution. Thousands of unconnected wallets holding small
amounts. High confidence in the "wisdom of the crowd."
● High Score (80-100): Highly concentrated. A few clusters control the liquidity. High risk
of manipulation or "rug pull" behavior (e.g., a whale dumping their entire position,

crashing the odds).
5.3 Integrating "Time Travel"
Just as Bubble Maps allows users to see token distribution at a past block height, a
Polymarket tool should offer "Time Travel" for odds.
● Feature: Replay the order book during a specific news event.
● Use Case: A trader wants to analyze how the "Trump vs. Harris" odds reacted
second-by-second during a debate. The tool replays the accumulation of shares,
highlighting which clusters bought before the price moved. This identifies "Insider"
wallets.18
6. Blueprint II: The "News Sniper" Terminal
The second major untapped market is high-frequency "News Trading." In memecoins, bots
snipe liquidity; in prediction markets, they snipe information.
6.1 The "ConditionPreparation" Listener
Markets on Polymarket are created via the ConditionPreparation event.8
● The Strategy: Often, a market is created for an event that is already occurring or where
the outcome is obvious to a specialist but not yet priced in by the general public.
● The Tool: A real-time scanner that listens for new QuestionInitialized events from the
UMA adapter. It uses LLMs (Large Language Models) to parse the ancillaryData (the
question text) and categorize it immediately (e.g., "Sports - NBA - Lakers Game").
● The Edge: The tool alerts the user (or auto-trades) the moment liquidity is added,
allowing them to buy "YES" at $0.50 for a game where the Lakers are already up by 20
points, before the broader market corrects the price to $0.90.
6.2 Latency Arbitrage Architecture
To build this as a paid service, the developer must solve the latency problem.
● Architecture:
○ Node Infrastructure: Run a dedicated Polygon RPC node to listen to the mempool.
○ CLOB WebSocket: Maintain a persistent connection to the Polymarket relayer
wss://ws-live-data.polymarket.com.19
○ Zero-Block Execution: Use a "Hot Wallet" residing on a server closer to the relayer
to sign and submit orders faster than web-based users.
6.3 Automated "News Trading" Bots
Integrate with news APIs (e.g., Bloomberg, AP, Twitter firehose).
● Workflow:

1. Trigger: AP reports "Fed Cuts Rates by 50bps."
2. LLM Agent: Parses headline -> Matches to Polymarket Event "Fed Rate Cut > 25bps".
3. Action: Bot auto-buys "YES" shares via the CLOB API.
4. Value: Humans are too slow. A "News-to-Execution" bot service is a high-ticket SaaS
product ($500+/mo) for institutional traders.20
7. Blueprint III: The Cross-Market Arbitrage Engine
The "Holy Grail" of paid tools is risk-free arbitrage. With the re-entry of Polymarket into the US
(via compliant apps) and the existence of competitors like Kalshi, price discrepancies are
inevitable due to market segmentation.22
7.1 Polymarket vs. Kalshi Scanner
These two platforms often price the same event differently because their user bases cannot
easily cross-pollinate (US Regulated vs. Global Crypto).
● The Discrepancy: Kalshi (US Only) might price "Inflation > 3%" at 40 cents. Polymarket
(Global) might price it at 35 cents.
● The Tool: A real-time "Arb Finder" dashboard.23
○ Normalization Engine: Convert Kalshi's % odds and Polymarket's USDC prices into a
standardized format.
○ Fee Calculator: Crucially, the tool must deduct fees. Kalshi charges ~0.7% to 1% in
trading fees. Polymarket has gas fees and withdrawal friction.24 The tool must display
the Net Arb Spread.
○ Liquidity Check: A spread is useless if there is no depth. The tool must check the
order book depth on both sides to calculate the "Max Tradable Size" before the arb
closes.
7.2 Negative Risk Arbitrage Scanner
Polymarket supports "Negative Risk" markets (mutually exclusive outcomes).
● The Math: In a 5-horse race, the sum of "YES" prices should equal $1.00 (plus/minus
spread).
● The Opportunity: Often, retail exuberance pushes the sum to $1.05 or $1.10.
● The Tool: A background scanner that continuously sums the "Best Ask" prices of all
outcomes in a Negative Risk group.
○ Alert: "Sum > 1.02 detected."
○ Strategy: The user sells "YES" on all outcomes (effectively acting as the house),
locking in a risk-free 2% profit.
○ Automation: This can be fully automated via a bot, as it relies purely on internal
Polymarket mathematics, removing the need for cross-chain bridging.9

8. Data Engineering: The Technical Moat
Building these tools is not merely a UI challenge; it is a data engineering challenge. The
"untapped" value lies in cleaning and structuring data that is currently messy.
8.1 Dealing with Rate Limits
Polymarket's API is robust but rate-limited.25
● Constraint: 5000 requests/10s for general data; 2400 requests/10s for trading.
● Solution for Paid Tools: Do not let user clients query Polymarket directly. Build a
Middleware Caching Layer.
○ The Service (Backend) queries Polymarket and caches the Order Book state in Redis.
○ User Clients connect to the Service via WebSocket.
○ Benefit: This allows the tool to serve thousands of users without hitting Polymarket's
IP rate limits, a critical scalability feature for a SaaS product.
8.2 Identity Resolution: EOA vs. Proxy
● The Challenge: Users trade via Proxy Wallets (Gnosis Safe), but fund them via EOAs
(Externally Owned Accounts).
● The Solution: The tool must index ProxyDeployed events to map EOAs to Proxies.
● Feature: "Whale Portfolio View." Show the user not just what the Whale's proxy holds, but
trace the funds back to the EOA to see what other assets they hold (e.g., do they hold a
lot of Trump-themed memecoins on Solana?). This cross-chain identity resolution is
currently non-existent in prediction markets.26
9. Monetization Strategy: The Business of Alpha
How does one monetize these "adjacent" tools? The memecoin market offers proven models.
9.1 The "Taxed Token" Model
Launch a utility token for the tool.
● Mechanism: Users must hold $100 worth of $TOOL tokens to access the "Whale
Cluster" charts.
● Revenue: A tax (e.g., 5%) on trading the $TOOL token funds development. This aligns the
community with the tool's success (as seen with Bubblemaps' $BMT token).27
9.2 The Subscription SaaS Model
● Tier 1 (Free): Basic charts, PnL tracking (Survivor Bias free).
● Tier 2 ($50/mo): "Smart Money" wallet alerts, Arb Scanner (delayed 15 mins).
● Tier 3 ($200/mo): Real-time "Event Sniper" feed, "Consensus Cluster" visualization, Live

Arbitrage signals.
9.3 The "Builder" Revenue Share
Polymarket offers a "Builders Program" where developers can earn a share of volume or
grants for routing trades.10
● Strategy: Build the tool as a trading terminal (like Photon). If users trade through your UI,
you claim "Order Attribution."
● Potential: High-volume terminals can earn significant revenue from the protocol itself,
reducing the need to charge users directly and accelerating adoption.
10. Regulatory and Ethical Considerations
10.1 The Oracle Risk
A paid tool must protect its users from "Bad Resolutions."
● Risk: UMA oracles can be disputed. A market might resolve "Incorrectly" in the eyes of
the trader due to technicalities in the ancillaryData.
● Tool Feature: "Resolution Risk Score." Analyze the text of the market question. If it
contains ambiguous terms (e.g., "significantly," "soon"), flag it as "High Dispute Risk."
● Value: Saving a user from locking capital in a 2-week dispute is worth the subscription
price alone.
10.2 U.S. Compliance
With the launch of a compliant U.S. app, the ecosystem is bifurcating.29
● Strategy: Build the analytics tool to be jurisdiction-agnostic (analyzing global data), but
restrict execution features based on user IP.
● Opportunity: Be the bridge. Offer a dashboard that shows "Global Sentiment"
(Polymarket) to US traders (Kalshi) and vice versa. Information is not regulated; only
execution is.
11. Conclusion: The "Bloomberg for Truth"
The prediction market ecosystem is currently in its "dial-up" phase. The capital is there, the
interest is there, but the tools are slow, manual, and opaque. The "untapped market" is not
simply a better wallet tracker; it is a comprehensive Market Intelligence & Execution
Platform.
By adapting the "memecoin standard" of tooling—specifically Visual Forensics (Bubble
Maps) to detect manipulation, Sniping (Photon) to capture early liquidity, and Arbitrage
Scanning to exploit market inefficiencies—developers can build a highly lucrative, paid
service. The demand for "Alpha" in prediction markets is no different from the demand in

equities or crypto; traders will always pay for speed, clarity, and an unfair advantage. The first
developer to provide a "Cluster Map of Truth" for Polymarket's conditional tokens will define
the next generation of InfoFi infrastructure.
