# Standard Billing Rates and Billable Activities

## Billable Time Categories

Attorneys may bill for the following activities at their standard hourly rate:

- Client consultations (in-person, telephonic, video call)
- Legal research and analysis
- Drafting of pleadings, agreements, opinions, and correspondence
- Attendance at court, arbitration, or mediation
- Preparation time for court appearances or client meetings
- File review and matter administration (maximum 30 minutes per matter per month)
- Reviewing correspondence and documents received from third parties

## Non-Billable Activities

The following may NOT be billed to clients:

- Internal firm administrative tasks
- New matter setup and conflict checks
- Billing disputes and account queries
- Training and professional development time

## Time Recording Rules

- All time must be recorded in 6-minute (0.1 hour) increments
- Minimum billing unit per activity: 0.2 hours (12 minutes)
- Maximum billable preparation time per court appearance: 3 hours without partner approval
- Telephone calls under 6 minutes: bill at 0.1 hour flat rate
